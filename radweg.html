Nuremberg Prague Cycle Highway!

20th April 2023 7 min read

Cycling enthusiasts, nature lovers, and adventure seekers rejoice! The Nuremberg-Prague Cycle Highway, also known as the Greenway, is a breathtakingly beautiful and well-designed cycling route that spans across two countries, offering an unforgettable experience for cyclists of all skill levels. This 380-kilometer-long route connects the historic cities of Nuremberg in Germany and Prague in the Czech Republic, passing through picturesque landscapes, quaint villages, and fascinating cultural sites along the way. Let's take a closer look at what makes the Nuremberg-Prague Cycle Highway a must-visit destination for cycling enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

The Nuremberg-Prague Cycle Highway is a well-maintained and fully signposted cycling route that follows a mix of dedicated cycling paths, quiet country roads, and scenic trails. The route is suitable for all types of cyclists, from families with young children to experienced long-distance cyclists. The majority of the route is paved, making it a comfortable and enjoyable ride for cyclists of all levels. The entire route is divided into five stages, each offering its unique highlights and attractions.

The journey begins in Nuremberg, a historic city in Germany known for its medieval architecture, rich cultural heritage, and famous Christmas market. From Nuremberg, cyclists pedal their way through the Franconian Lake District, a region dotted with beautiful lakes, lush forests, and charming Bavarian villages. As cyclists venture further into the Czech Republic, they are greeted with stunning landscapes, including the beautiful Bohemian Forest, known as Šumava in Czech. This national park is a pristine wilderness area with deep forests, glacial lakes, and rugged peaks, offering a perfect backdrop for a memorable cycling adventure.

The route also takes cyclists through numerous picturesque towns and villages, allowing them to immerse themselves in the local culture and enjoy the warm hospitality of the people. One such highlight is the medieval town of Pilsen, famous for its Pilsner beer and well-preserved historic architecture, including the magnificent Pilsen Cathedral. Another must-visit destination is the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Český Krumlov, a fairy-tale-like town with narrow cobblestone streets, Renaissance buildings, and a stunning castle perched on a hill overlooking the Vltava River.

One of the major highlights of the Nuremberg-Prague Cycle Highway is the opportunity to explore the rich history and cultural heritage of the region. The route passes through numerous historical sites, including castles, fortresses, and ruins, which offer glimpses into the region's rich past. For history buffs, a visit to the medieval castle of Karlštejn, located near Prague, is a must. This iconic castle, founded in the 14th century by Charles IV, is known for its impressive Gothic architecture and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Apart from its historical and cultural attractions, the Nuremberg-Prague Cycle Highway also offers an abundance of natural beauty. The route meanders through lush forests, serene lakes, and tranquil rivers, providing ample opportunities for cyclists to connect with nature and enjoy the great outdoors. Cycling through the Bohemian Forest, with its pristine wilderness and unique ecosystem, is a particularly memorable experience. The route also passes through the picturesque Šumava National Park, which is home to rare plant and animal species, making it a paradise for nature lovers.
