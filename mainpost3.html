Gravel Cycling Adventures: Exploring the Old Canal of Nuremberg

10th Feb 2023 7 min read

If you're a cycling enthusiast seeking a unique and adventurous experience, look no further than the old canal of Nuremberg in Germany. This hidden gem offers gravel cycling enthusiasts a chance to explore a historic waterway while immersing themselves in the natural beauty and rich history of the region. From scenic trails to fascinating landmarks, gravel cycling along the old canal of Nuremberg promises an unforgettable adventure for riders of all skill levels.

The old canal of Nuremberg, also known as the Ludwig-Donau-Main-Kanal, was constructed in the 19th century as a transportation route to connect the Danube, Main, and Rhine rivers. Today, the canal has been decommissioned and transformed into a recreational area that spans over 170 kilometers, offering a variety of gravel cycling routes for outdoor enthusiasts to explore.

One of the highlights of gravel cycling along the old canal of Nuremberg is the opportunity to soak in the picturesque landscapes of the Franconian countryside. The canal is flanked by lush green fields, tranquil forests, and charming villages, providing a peaceful and scenic backdrop for your cycling adventure. The gravel trails along the canal are well-maintained, making for smooth and enjoyable riding, with minimal traffic and distractions, allowing you to fully immerse yourself in the serenity of nature as you pedal along.

If you're up for a challenge, the old canal of Nuremberg also offers some exciting off-road sections for more advanced gravel cyclists. These sections feature rugged terrain, including gravel paths, forest trails, and challenging climbs, providing a thrilling experience for adrenaline-seeking riders. Just be sure to come prepared with the right gear, including a sturdy gravel bike, helmet, and appropriate safety equipment, and always ride within your skill level.
